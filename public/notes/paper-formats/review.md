## 1. Introduction  
Establish the intellectual context and justify the need for the review.

**Content:**  
- Background and evolution of the research area  
- Motivation for conducting the review (e.g., fragmentation, rapid growth, conflicting findings)  
- Distinction from existing surveys or reviews  
- Clear articulation of the review‚Äôs objectives and contributions  
- Outline of the paper‚Äôs structure  


## 2. Scope and Review Methodology  
Ensure transparency and rigor in literature selection and synthesis.

**Content:**  
- Criteria for inclusion and exclusion of studies  
- Databases, time span, and types of sources considered  
- Screening and filtering strategy  
- Limitations of the review process  

*Note:* This section emphasizes systematic reasoning rather than experimental protocols.


## 3. Conceptual Framework or Taxonomy  
Provide a unifying structure for organizing and interpreting the literature.

**Content:**  
- Definition of core concepts and terminology  
- Development of a classification scheme (e.g., by methodology, assumptions, objectives, or paradigms)  
- Justification for the chosen taxonomy  

The framework introduced here should guide the synthesis throughout the paper.


## 4. Synthesis of Existing Literature  
Integrate prior work into coherent themes rather than listing individual studies.

**Content:**  
- Thematic or category-wise discussion aligned with the proposed taxonomy  
- Comparative analysis across approaches  
- Identification of dominant patterns, consensus, and divergences  
- Evolution of ideas over time  

The emphasis is on relationships, abstractions, and insights.



## 5. Comparative and Critical Analysis  
Evaluate strengths and weaknesses of existing approaches at a conceptual or methodological level.

**Content:**  
- Comparative discussion across categories or paradigms  
- Trade-offs, assumptions, and constraints  
- Contexts in which certain approaches are effective or limited  
- Cross-cutting challenges identified across the literature  


## 6. Limitations and Open Challenges  
Highlight unresolved issues and structural weaknesses in the body of literature.

**Content:**  
- Methodological, theoretical, or practical limitations  
- Gaps in coverage, evaluation, or generalization  
- Underexplored directions or neglected perspectives  

This section should remain analytical rather than speculative.


## 7. Emerging Trends and Future Research Directions  
Provide informed guidance on how the field is evolving and where future work is needed.

**Content:**  
- Emerging themes or shifts in research focus  
- Promising methodological or conceptual directions  
- Recommendations grounded in identified gaps and trends  

Predictions should be cautious and evidence-based.


## 8. Implications and Broader Impact  
Discuss the significance of the synthesized insights beyond individual studies.

**Content:**  
- Implications for theory, methodology, or practice  
- Relevance to related disciplines or interdisciplinary research  
- How the review advances understanding of the field as a whole  


## 9. Conclusion  
Reinforce the contribution and lasting value of the review.

**Content:**  
- Recap of key synthesized insights  
- Reflection on the review‚Äôs scope and limitations  
- Final remarks on the state and trajectory of the field  


## References  
Provide a comprehensive and authoritative bibliographic foundation.

**Content:**  
Well-curated, relevant, and balanced citations, formatted according to the target venue‚Äôs guidelines.
